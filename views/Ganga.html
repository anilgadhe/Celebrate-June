🌊 गंगा दशहरा - Ganga Dussehra

The sacred descent of the divine Ganga — purifying both land and soul.

When is it Celebrated?

On the 10th day (Dashami) of the waxing moon in the Jyeshtha month (usually June)

🕉️ Significance

According to Hindu belief, on this auspicious day, the holy river Ganga descended from the heavens to Earth to purify the souls of mankind. King Bhagirath’s relentless penance led to this celestial event to cleanse his ancestors’ sins. This event is revered across India — especially in Maharashtra where river rituals are integral to the spiritual culture.

💧 Marathi Traditions & Emotions

  • Devotees bathe in nearby rivers — Godavari, Krishna, or other local streams — symbolizing soul purification.
  • Families offer Gangajal at home temples, believing it brings purity, peace, and protection.
  • Bhajans, Haripath readings, and storytelling of Bhagirath’s faith echo through village mandirs.
  • Children float diyas (lamps) in rivers, watching their light dance over flowing water, carrying wishes downstream.

Aarti, Arghya, and Abhishek — the soul cleanses as the river flows 🌊
